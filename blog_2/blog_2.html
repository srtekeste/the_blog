Good Farms

We turn our gaze towards another contender from the vibrant fields of Mexico. This batch, sporting a regular sticker price of $8.99, caught my eye not only for its origin but also for the promising sale offer of two dollars off. Upon closer inspection, these strawberries presented a striking visual appeal, they boasted a vibrant ruby red hue. However, nestled at their tops were patches of white and green--a visual cue that perhaps these berries had been plucked from the vine just shy of reaching their full potential. The taste test revealed a mixed bag of impressions. These strawberries were decidedly firmer than the previous ones I had tasted from other brands, including the first two I had encountered. This firmness was not unpleasant but was a noticeable departure from the soft, juicy burst one might expect.
The flavor, while not off-putting, was kind of ‘meh’--lacking the sweetness that had set the Sweet Success berries apart as a benchmark. Size-wise, these strawberries were the epitome of consistency, each berry roughly mirroring the next in dimensions comparable to that of a walnut. This uniformity, often a marker of quality and care in selection, was commendable. Despite their pleasing appearance and uniform size, the taste left something desired. They could have benefitted from a bit more time to develop that rich sweetness that is the hallmark of a truly great strawberry. Would I buy them again? In a pinch, yes. If these were the best option amidst possibly less appealing choices, they’d find their way into my shopping cart. However, they wouldn’t be my first pick off the shelf.
